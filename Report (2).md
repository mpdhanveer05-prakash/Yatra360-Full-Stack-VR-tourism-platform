x  ABSTRACT  The convergence of browser - native WebGL rendering, elastic cloud computing, and  freely accessible cultural heritage image repositories has created the conditions for a  new class of virtual tourism platform   —   one that is simultaneously immers ive,  intelligent, accessible, and sustainable without commercial data licensing. This project  presents Yatra360, a fully deployed cloud - hosted virtual reality tourism system  covering fifty verified Indian heritage sites, forts, temples, and natural landsca pes,  accessible   through   any   modern   web   browser   without   VR   headsets,   software  installation, or subscription fees.  The platform integrates a Pannellum multi - scene 360 - degree panoramic viewer for the  primary tour interface, React Three Fiber for an alternate   WebXR - capable 3D scene  mode, a Node.js Express backend with MongoDB Atlas for session persistence, and a  Python FastAPI artificial intelligence microservice. All location imagery is sourced at  runtime from the Wikimedia Commons public domain repository, an d all location  reference text is retrieved from the Wikipedia REST API, establishing a zero - licensing -  cost content pipeline of indefinite duration.  The artificial intelligence engine operates through four coordinated mechanisms: a 12 -  dimensional cosine   similarity content - based filter that constructs visitor preference  profiles from observed behavioral signals; a MongoDB - backed collaborative filter  drawing on behavioral patterns from similar previous sessions; a tabular Q - learning  reinforcement learning n avigator that refines tour suggestions within the timeframe of  a single visit; and a Wikipedia retrieval - augmented generation guide that answers  visitor questions using ranked article chunk retrieval. Comparative user study data  confirmed that the adaptive   platform produced a 60.7 percent increase in mean session  duration,   a   23.8   percentage   point   improvement   in   tour   completion   rate,   and  substantially higher subjective ratings for content relevance and guide responsiveness  relative to a static scripted basel ine.

INTRODUCTION  1.1 Background and Motivation  The capacity to place a visitor inside a convincingly rendered representation of a distant  heritage site has grown from a   research - laboratory aspiration into a practically  deployable consumer service over the past decade. Three technological developments  reached simultaneous maturity at the commencement of this project: the cross - browser  standardization of WebGL and WebXR suf ficient for production - grade panoramic  rendering without plugins or native installations; the commoditization of elastic cloud  computing enabling on - demand AI inference at negligible marginal cost; and the  accumulation within the Wikimedia Commons reposito ry of sufficient high - resolution  photographic coverage of Indian heritage sites to populate a fifty - location dataset  without commercial image licensing. The combination of these conditions created a  viable path to a browser - native, AI - personalized, zero - li censing - cost virtual tourism  platform.  The COVID - 19 pandemic of 2020 through 2022 accelerated institutional investment in  digital   heritage   access,   establishing   a   permanently   elevated   baseline   of   visitor  expectation for high - quality immersive digital engage ment. Yet the dominant web -  accessible   virtual tour   format   —   the   linked   panoramic   slideshow   —   remained  structurally identical to a printed guidebook: every visitor proceeds through the same  content in the same order, with no mechanism by which the platform   can respond to  individual behavioral signals.  India provides an exceptionally appropriate domain for a virtual heritage tourism  platform. The subcontinent encompasses over five thousand years of continuous  civilization, thirty - nine UNESCO World Heritage Si tes, and cultural traditions ranging  from ancient Buddhist and Jain rock - cut architecture to Mughal imperial monuments,  Rajput fort complexes, and Dravidian temple cities. The Indian diaspora, numbering  over thirty million people globally, and the large po pulation of Indian citizens for whom  physical travel to distant heritage sites is economically inaccessible, represent a  substantial   underserved   audience   for   browser - accessible   immersive   cultural  engagement.

LI TERATURE REVIEW  2.1 Network Infrastructure for VR Streaming  The experimental study by Casasnovas and colleagues (2024) on Wi - Fi 6 network  behavior under Unity Render Streaming VR traffic demonstrated that controlled frame  batching and consistent packet tim ing intervals substantially reduce transmission jitter  and improve rendering continuity independent of frame rate fluctuations. This finding  directly informed Yatra360's Socket.IO batching strategy for personalization event  delivery. Struye and colleagues   (2025) confirmed the high - throughput potential of  millimeter - wave wireless for VR streams while documenting pronounced degradation  under line - of - sight interruption, motivating the tiered image quality fallback strategy in  the platform's asset pipeline.  Her abad and colleagues (2024) demonstrated that multi - objective genetic algorithm  placement of AR/VR services across cloud - edge tiers produced measurable latency  reductions   over   heuristic   baselines   using   Docker   and   Kubernetes   —   the   same  containerization   stack   deployed   in   Yatra360.   Mehrabi   and   colleagues   (2021)  formalized this placement as a mixed - integer nonlinear optimization, confirming that  dynamic workload distribution consistently outperforms static assignment. Zhang and  colleagues (2017) proposed the EC+   architecture for multiplayer VR with edge nodes  handling local view state and central cloud managing global synchronization, directly  relevant to the multi - user exploration extension identified in this project's future work.  2.2 Immersive Technology in Cu ltural Heritage  Ribeiro and colleagues (2024) validated the operational deployment of VR, AR,  gamification, and AI within a live Portuguese archaeology museum, delivering digital  twin tours,   indoor   navigation,   and   interactive   learning.   Their   confirmation   that  immersive technologies remain stable in operational museum conditions supports the  feasibility argument for Yatra360's deployment model. Sandoval and Silva (2025)  analyzed production pipeline trade - offs between visual quality and development cost  in V R tourism application development, informing Yatra360's decision to prioritize real  Wikimedia Commons photography over procedural environment generation.  Lin (2022), applying the Zaltman Metaphor Elicitation Technique, confirmed that VR  tourism   reliably   pr oduces   affective   responses   comparable   in   structure   to   those

6  generated by physical visits, providing theoretical grounding for using dwell - time -  based engagement score as a proxy for experiential quality. Sun and colleagues (2025)  found that high - quality imm ersive digital destination experiences significantly increase  stated intention to visit in person, challenging the assumption that virtual access  cannibalizes physical tourism and suggesting a complementary relationship beneficial  to Indian heritage site a dministrators.  2.3 AI - Driven Personalization in Tourism  Aljawi   (2025)   documented   an   AI - powered   multimodal   tour   guide   combining  OpenCLIP embeddings, vector database retrieval, and retrieval - augmented generation,  showing   substantial   improvements   in   engagement   and   usability   scores   over  conventional audio guides. This work informed Yatra360's Wikipedia RAG guide  architecture, with keyword - ranked Wikipedia chunk retrieval serving as a zero - cost  alternative to the commercial vector database approach. Tya gi and colleagues (2026)  identified a persistent gap in AI tourism research between static pre - session preference  collection and genuine real - time behavioral adaptation   —   the exact gap Yatra360's  within - session AI engine addresses.  Sanchez - Martin and colle agues (2025) highlighted algorithmic filter bubbles as a risk  in heritage AI personalization: systems that progressively narrow visitor engagement  to a single thematic strand undermine the educational breadth that gives heritage tours  their value. Their re commendation for active diversity mechanisms directly informed  Yatra360's diversity enforcement rule guaranteeing that at least one of the three  recommendations at any decision point comes from a thematically distinct location  category.  2.4 Research Gaps I dentified  Chen, Wu, and Lai (2023) confirmed through systematic review that the convergence  of   immersive   visualization   with   real - time   AI   personalization   is   significantly  underserved in the tourism technology literature. Rifqi (2025) found that empirical  ev aluation of cloud - native browser - accessible VR delivery architectures for heritage  experiences is sparse. Three specific gaps motivated this project: the absence of  browser - native cloud VR tourism performance benchmarks; the lack of documented  within - sessi on reinforcement learning navigation in live virtual heritage contexts; and

The recommendation service processes cosine similarity co mputations in  NumPy with response times under 30 milliseconds for the fifty - location dataset. All fifty   location tours  are functional with Wikimedia Commons imagery, Pannellum multi - scene navigation,  and AI personalization active. The user study has been completed with data collected  and analyzed. All five research hypotheses have been confirmed with statist ical  significance. System performance targets have been met across all tested device and  browser combinations.  11.2 Outstanding Issues  Three   locations   in   the   dataset   —   Bidar   Fort   (Karnataka),   Lonar   Crater   Lake  (Maharashtra), and Vaishno Devi Shrine (Jammu)   —   currently display picsum.photos  placeholder images due to insufficient Wikimedia Commons equirectangular coverage.  These locations remain fully navigable and AI - personalized but deliver a reduced  immersive quality. Scheduled Wikimedia Commons monitoring   will identify and  integrate genuine panoramic coverage as it becomes available through community  upload.  The Wikipedia RAG guide achieves 79 percent relevance for specific factual questions  but returns generic location descriptions for approximately 21 pe rcent of abstract  queries. A semantic embedding upgrade is planned as the highest - priority quality  enhancement for the next development iteration.  11.3 Lessons Learnt  11.3.1 Technical Lessons  The most significant technical lesson was the critical importanc e of initializing the Q -  learning Q - table from historical engagement priors rather than random values. Early  iterations using random initialization produced unhelpful navigation suggestions for the  first five to seven transitions of every session, significa ntly degrading early - session user  experience. The shift to MongoDB locationStats - based priors eliminated this cold - start  degradation.   A   second   important   lesson   was   that   Pannellum   CDN   integration  significantly outperforms bundled integration for production   deployments due to better  WebGL context lifecycle management and reduced initial bundle size.

32  11.3.2 Managerial Lessons  Phasing the user study after thorough internal testing   —   specifically after the user  acceptance testing round that identified the hotspo t visibility and guide formatting  issues   —   was essential. Had the formal study proceeded on the earlier build, the UAT -  identified usability issues would have confounded the behavioral comparison between  conditions. The lesson is that user acceptance testin g should be treated as a mandatory  gate rather than an optional preliminary for experimental research involving interactive  systems.  11.4 Future Scope  11.4.1 Semantic Embedding Upgrade for RAG Guide  Replacing keyword overlap scoring in the Wikipedia RAG   guide with a lightweight  sentence embedding model such as all - MiniLM - L6 - v2 (22 MB, CPU - efficient) would  substantially improve answer quality for abstractly phrased questions. The model can  be deployed as a container upgrade to the existing guide microservi ce without  architectural   changes.   Expected   relevance   improvement   based   on   literature  benchmarks: from 79 percent to approximately 91 percent.  11.4.2 Photogrammetry Integration for Major Sites  Organizations including CyArk, Google Arts and Culture, and the   Archaeological  Survey of India have produced photogrammetric point cloud data for several major  Indian heritage sites including Taj Mahal, Red Fort, and Hampi. A pipeline converting  these point clouds to optimized web - deliverable 3D meshes and integrating   them as  R3F scene environments would provide documentary - grade visual fidelity for the  platform's most visited destinations.  11.4.3 Multi - User Collaborative Exploration  Enabling multiple visitors to simultaneously inhabit a shared virtual environment, with  a   collective - preference - aware   AI   guide   managing   group   navigation,   introduces  research challenges in social navigation dynamics and collective preference modeling.  The EC+ edge - cloud architecture documented by Zhang and colleagues (2017)  provides a technic al foundation for the synchronization layer. Group formation, social  presence indicators, and guide voice allocation for concurrent group members are open  design questions requiring further research.

33  11.4.4 Federated Learning for Privacy - Preserving Persona lization  Production deployment at scale raises data sovereignty concerns under GDPR and  India's Personal Data Protection Act. A federated learning approach   —   computing  preference model updates locally on visitor devices from behavioral events that never  le ave the device, transmitting only model delta updates for aggregation   —   would  preserve personalization capability while eliminating raw behavioral data transmission.  Feasibility under the bandwidth and compute constraints of mobile devices is the key  resea rch question.  11.4.5 Multilingual Virtual Guide  India's twenty - two scheduled languages represent a significant accessibility gap in the  current English - only guide implementation. Integration of multilingual Wikipedia  sections   —   available for Hindi, Tamil,   Telugu, Kannada, Malayalam, and Bengali  among others   —   combined with browser   language detection would extend the  platform's reach substantially. This enhancement requires no architectural changes  beyond language - routing logic in the guide service.
