I NTRODUCTION   ( H EADING   1 )  The development of virtual reality (VR) technologies  opened up new opportunities to simulate the real world  environment to a greater extent by being more immersive. The  VR - based tourism platforms enable the user to explore the  three - dimensional space and m anipulate virtual items, which  produce a more significant feeling of presence than traditional  media. The world - wide COVID - 19 pandemic also enhanced  the embrace of such technologies. Travel restrictions, safety,  and lockdown measures led to a significant d rop in physical  tourism and both   travellers   and tourism providers began to  seek digital solutions. This meant that virtual tourism was a  good avenue of visiting cultural heritage sites, museums,  natural landscapes and historical sites without having to travel  physically. During   the post - pandemic er a,   the trend has  resulted in an increased demand in remote and accessible  tourism experiences; especially among the people with either  mobility, financial or geographical restrictions .  Regardless of these innovations, current virtual tourism  websites continue to have a number of drawbacks. Various  VR - based systems need some specialized hardware including  head - mounted   displays,   high - performing   computers,   and  specific software installations. Such demands make it more  expensive   to   adopt   and   make   the   implementation   less  accessible to a wider audience. On the contrary, virtual tours  that are done using the web   are more affordable and tend to  have little or no interactivity. These systems are n ormally  based on panoramic viewers or pre - defined navigation routes,  and it creates stagnant and straight line discovery experiences  that do not conform to personal user interests or   behavioural  patterns.  This study will solve these issues by introducing a virtual  tourism platform based on the cloud platform to provide users  with immersive experience by directly using ordinary web  browsers. Using the latest web technologies, including Web  XR and cloud technology, the system does not require special  VR devices and still allows interactivity and rich visual  experiences. The users are able to use virtual destinations with  a variety of devices such as desktops, laptops, tablets, and  smartphones   which enhances accessibility and scalability.  One of the main aspects of the suggested platform is that  it is built on artificial intelligence and allows adaptive and  personal exploration. The system keeps track of the interaction  of the user, such as patterns of viewing, navigation as well as  frequen cy of visiting certain places. On the basis of this data,  the platform rearranges the order of destinations dynamically  and suggests the appropriate locations in real - time. Such a  dynamic navigation system alters the old linear tour model  into an interacti ve and user oriented exploration experience.  The main aim of the work is to develop and introduce a  scalable   architecture   that   includes   the   cloud   computing,  immersive   web   technologies,   and   intelligent   methods of  personalization.   The platform   can   reliably   provide   high -  quality immersive content when of floading computationally  intensive tasks and heavy media assets to the cloud servers  without having to demand a considerable processing power on  the client machine.   The originality of the suggested solution is  in   the   integration   of   immersive   environments   based   on  browsers and adaptive tour generation in real - time. In contrast  to   traditional   virtual   tourism   platforms,   which   display  predetermined   navigation   routes,   the   suggested   platform  constantly analyses users to make changes to the tour flow and  suit personal preferences and patterns of engagement.

II.   L ITERATURE   R EVIEW  Network   performance   plays   a   pivotal   role   in   VR  streaming. Research investigating the interplay between Wi -  Fi 6 and VR traffic demonstrated that packetization strategies  and controlled frame batching improve transmission stability  for edge - to - user streaming   using Unity Render Streaming and  WebRTC. Experimental validation confirmed that consistent  packet intervals reduce jitter and enhance rendering continuity  across varying frame rates   [1] . Complementing this, studies  exploring   mm Wave   wireless links highlighted their ability to  deliver   high - throughput   VR   streams   but   also   revealed  significant degradation in performance under line - of - sight  blockages,   indicating   reliability   concerns in   mobile   user  scenarios   [2] .  To address latency constraints, multiple works explored  edge - to - cloud   architectures.   One   study   proposed   optimal  service   placement   across   multi - tier   infrastructures   using  Docker,   Kubernetes,   and   a   Multi - Objective   Genetic  Algorithm. The approach effectively   minimized latency and  improved   reliability   compared   to heuristic   baselines   [3] .  Similarly, another study formulated workload distribution  between cloud and edge servers as a mixed - integer nonlinear  optimization   problem,   enabling   dynamic   allocation   of  rendering tasks. Simulation results using 5G and   Simulate  demonstrated improved video quality and reduced latency  compared to random placement strategies   [4] . Extending this  paradigm, an EC+ architecture for massively multiplayer VR  games leveraged edge nodes for local view updates and central  cloud   servers for   global   state   management,   significantly  reducing view - change latency and backbone traffic   [5] .  Beyond   networking,   immersive   technologies   are  increasingly   applied   in   tourism   and   cultural   heritage.   A  framework integrating AR, VR, AI, and gamification was  deployed in a Portuguese archaeology museum to provide  digital twin tours, indoor navigation, and   interactive learning.  The   prototype   successfully   validated   the   feasibility   of  immersive technologies in cultural preservation and education  [6] . In tourism development contexts, VR applications built  using engines such as Unity and Unreal Engine enable virtual  destination previews and historical reconstructions, enhancing  user engagement and marketing effectiveness   [7] .  Several   studies   focused   on   user   psychology   and  behavioural   outcomes of VR experiences. Research using the  Zaltman Metaphor Elicitation Technique revealed that VR  tourism evokes strong emotional and cognitive responses,  influencing user perception of destinations   [8] . Similarly,  empirical   modelling   based   on   the   Theory   of   Planned  Behaviour   demonstrated that immersive digital experiences  positively   influence   tourists’   intention   to   visit   physical  destinations   [9] . Multisensory VR setups were also found to  significantly   enhance   presence   and   emotional   immersion  compared to conventional VR, as validated through controlled  experiments and presence questionnaires   [10] . Furthermore,  studies on ecological virtual environments indicated that  immersive   experiences   can   encourage   environmentally  responsible   behaviour   among tourists   [11] .  A substantial portion of the literature comprises systematic  and bibliometric reviews, highlighting research trends and  gaps. Analyses using PRISMA guidelines and bibliometric  tools such as   VOS viewer   identified major clusters in AR/VR  tourism   research,   including   destination   marketing,   user  experience, and technology adoption   [12] ,   [13] . Another  bibliometric study focusing on AI in tourism marketing  mapped thematic evolution over two decades, revealing a shift  toward smart tourism ecosystems and personalized digital  services   [14] .   Additional   reviews   emphasized   the  transformative role of AI in heritage tourism, particularly in  recommendation   systems,   computer   vision – based   artifact  recognition, and ethical data usage   [15] .  AI - driven   personalization   further   enhances   immersive  tourism   platforms.   A   multimodal   AI   tour   guide   system  combining   Open CLIP   embeddings, vector databases, and  retrieval - augmented   generation   dynamically   produced  context - aware   storytelling,   significantly   improving   user  engagement and usability scores   [16] . Conceptual studies also  reinforced the synergy between VR, AR, and AI across the  tourist journey — from pre - trip planning to on - site navigation  and post - visit engagement — demonstrating a paradigm shift  toward fully digital, interactive travel ecosystems   [17] .  Post - pandemic   tourism   recovery   has   accelerated   AR  adoption, particularly through marker - based and location -  based systems designed to enable safe, contactless exploration  of destinations   [18] . Collectively, the literature indicates a  strong   convergence   between   immersive   visualization,  intelligent   content   generation,   and   advanced   networking  infrastructure.  In summary, the reviewed studies demonstrate that low -  latency   edge   computing,   high - bandwidth   wireless  technologies, and AI - driven personalization are foundational  to scalable VR and AR systems. While significant progress  has been made in improving network   efficiency, service  placement, and user immersion, challenges remain in ensuring  reliability under mobility, managing computational costs, and  addressing   ethical   considerations   in   AI - driven   tourism  platforms.   These   findings   underscore   the   importance   of  int egrated,   cross - disciplinary   research   in   advancing  immersive   digital   experiences   across   both   technical   and  application domains .  III.   AI - P OWERED   P ERSONALIZATION   E NGIN E  The   success   of   the   virtual tourism   platform   can   be  significantly influenced by its capacity to respond to the  interests and behavior of an individual user.   To conclude , the  suggested   system   will   incorporate   an   AI - driven  personalization engine that examines user interaction with the  system and modifies the tour experience on the fly. The  engine   uses   behavioral   analytics,   recommendation  algorithms and natural language pro cessing techniques to  provide context - based content and navigation routes. A more  interactive and personal exploration process is achievable by  the system as it constantly learns through the actions of the  user, unlike the traditional static virtual tours .  A.   Hybrid Recommendation Subsystem  The   recommendation   subsystem   will   constitute   an  important part of the personalization framework that will be  tasked with the responsibility of defining the most pertinent  locations, artifacts, or perspectives to include in a virtual tour.  The system has ut ilized a hybrid recommendation strategy that  integrates content - based and collaborative filtering strategy in  order   to   enhance   the   accuracy   and   diversity   of  recommendation. The content - based filtering is based on the  analysis   of   the   metadata,   including   his torical   relevance,  architectural style, and cultural context, to determine the

preferences of the users according to the interaction patterns.  Instead, collaborative filtering is a technique that tries to  compare session - to - session   behaviour   of users to identify  common   behaviour   patterns of exploration and indicate places  visited   by   similar   users.   The   combination   of   the   two  approaches will allow the system to make recommendations  remain relevant as well as expose the users to new and  potentially interesting destinations.  B.   AI - Based Virtual Guide  To improve user interaction, the platform also uses a  virtual guide, which helps in giving contextual explanations  and answering the query of users in real - time. When users are  exploring the virtual environment, they can pose questions  regarding the presen t place, its history, design, or cultural  significance. The query is processed by the system to retrieve  the appropriate metadata and produce brief answers to the  query so the tour is not just a passive viewing experience but  a whole new interactive and ed ucational experience. The  guide is also customized to the preferences of the users giving  more detailed descriptions of the art to the user interested in  the artistic side and technical information to the user interested  in the architectural or engineering   side, thus enhancing the  relevance and richness of the information given .  C.   Data Sources for Personalization  The personalization engine uses various sources of data to  make inferences about the user's preferences and   behaviour  patterns. Initial user profiles (potentially including choices of  interest categories during the registration process) are used to  provide   an   initial   baseline   for   generating   early  recommendations. However, the system relies mostly on real -  time data of int eraction gathered during exploration.  Interaction logs record things like how long you stay in  which place, how many times you interact with the hot spot,  what course you followed, how often you revisit it, etc. Longer  viewing times and repeated interactions are taken to mean  increased user in terest. By this analysis, the system builds a  dynamic model of user preference, which changes throughout  the session.  D.   Interest Heatmap Generation  In order to measure user activity throughout the virtual  environment, the system presents a mechanism of Interest  Heatmap. This intelligence layer gives recognition scores to  every virtual place on the basis of aggregated interaction  measures   such   as   dwell   time,   frequency   of   interaction,  direction of gaze and repetition of navigation.   Places with a  higher score in the engagement measure are regarded as places  with great user interest and those with a lower engagement  score depict low engagement. The heatmap generated is used  with two major purposes. It will help the recommendation  engin e give priority to locations based on the interests of the  user shown. Second, it offers administrators and content  designers a useful insight on what areas are most popular and  it is possible to optimize contents based on such insights.  E.   Adaptive Smart Tour Flow  The Adaptive Smart Tour Flow is one of the main  innovations   of   the   proposed   system   that   dynamically  rearranges the order of locations shown to the user. Classical  virtual tours are based on pre - programmed travelling routes  and   do   not   offer   much   customizati on   and   interaction.  Conversely, the exploration path is constantly adjusted in the  proposed   platform,   depending   on   the   behavioural   cues  provided in real - time.  Some of the user interest indicators checked by the system  include   dwell   time,   interaction   frequency,   zooming,   and  information request. The signals are utilized to approximate  the level of engagement and determine where users will most  probably be interest ed in. This means that every user is  provided with his or her own interacting tour track depending  on their preferences.  F.   Reinforcement Learning - Based Navigation Optimization  The RL model uses user interaction metrics as its input.  They are mouse movements, estimation of gaze, hotspots  clicks and time taken at each location. Referring to these  inputs, the agent calculates an engagement - based reward  function   which   expresses   the   user   satisfaction   and   the  intensity of interaction.  The transitions, which lead to increased engagement are  more rewarded and the agent is encouraged to take such a path  in the future. This system then gives the best next position and  it is smooth flowingly shown to the user with smooth camera  transitions o r emphasis of where to go. Due to the nature of  this process that runs throughout the session, the tour path may  dynamically change without a break in immersion.  IV.   S YSTEM   A RCHITECTURE AND   I MPLEMENTATION  The   proposed   virtual   tourism   platform   follows   a  cloud7XR architecture that is aimed at providing immersive  and interactive tourism experiences without requiring any  special software. The system does not require any special  virtual reality hardware and mass ive local installations by  decoupling visualization and computational processing. The  browser is the visualization interface and cloud infrastructure  is the processing and delivery of data, artificial intelligence  services   along   with   multimedia   assets.   Suc h   division  enhances   the   accessibility,   scalability   and   compatibility  across desktops, tablets, and smart phones .  A.   Architectural Overview  The platform is based on a layered architecture with four  major elements, which include the frontend interface, backend  orchestration   services,   AI   processing   modules   and   3D  rendering infrastructure. All the components are configured to  be   independent   servi ces   which   can   communicate   using  standardized APIs and real time messaging protocols. This  design can scale one and another layer individually depending  on the user need and the computation that is required.  The frontend layer is where the visualization is immersive  and all the interactions with the user are handled. Back - end  layer is involved in the communication between the client  interface, databases, and the AI services. The   behavioural   data  are   handled   by   the   AI   layer   to   produce   adaptive   tour  recommendations   whereas   the   rendering   infrastructure  handles storage and streaming of immersive 3D material .  B.   Web - Based Rendering and User Interface  The user interface is developed in HTML5, CSS and  JavaScript to allow compatibility with the contemporary  browsers as well as operating systems. WebGL is a high - level  library that is used to create three - dimensional environments

by offering scene management tools, lighting tools, camera  control tools, and object interaction tools (e.g. Three.js).  Web   XR is built into the platform so that users can explore  virtual environments without having to use VR headsets,  using the default input devices such as a mouse, touchscreen  or   device   orientation   sensors.   The   interface   would   be  developed based on the princi ples of responsive web design to  ensure that the interface would be functional and have its  layout across various screen sizes and device capabilities .  C.   Backend Services and Data Management  The implementation of the backend services is based on  the usage of Node.js which provides the support of the  asynchronous processing and the efficient work with the  concurrent   user   requests.   The   backend   deals   with   user  authentication,   session   tracking   and   interconnectedness  between the frontend and the cloud based services. Data on  interaction   formed   in   the   course   of   user   exploration   is  recorded and sent to the backend in real time to be further  processed.  The platform is built upon such technology as WebSocket -  based communication which is implemented using such a  technology as Socket.IO to create bidirectional connections  between the server and the client persistently. This facilitates  instant transfer of i nteraction data and real time update on  navigation advice.  A MongoDB database contains user profiles, a record of  interactions and metadata of virtual locations. Document  oriented nature of MongoDB enables the heterogeneous data  that is generated by virtual tour interaction to be stored  flexibly and at the same ti me enables the structure to scale to  high number of users .  D.   AI Processing and Adaptive Navigation Engine  The adaptive tour engine performs adjustment of the order  of presentation of locations to the user on a dynamical basis.  It is deployed as a collection of Python - based microservices  based on such frameworks as   Fast API . The format of the  navigation model is based on the Markov Decision Process  where every virtual location can be described as a state and  potential movements between locations as actions.  Engagement   scores   are   computed   based   on   user  interaction signals such as dwell time, hotspots interaction and  navigation patterns using a reward function. The engine  suggests and predicts, based on these scores, the most relevant  place next, in real - time,   and allows a personal and non - linear  route to explore .  E.   3D Content Management and Environment Construction  Virtual spaces are created with interconnected panoramic  areas or 3D space connected to each other with hotspots of  interaction.   Every   point   is   denoted   using   a   node   in   a  navigation graph, and the edges indicate any possible change  of viewpoint. Each node i s related to metadata that includes  contextual information e.g. historical context, architectural  data,   and   cultural   classification   to   assist   both   assistive  recommendation as well as contextual information retrieval .  F.   Cloud Deployment and Scalability  The application runs on cloud computing facilities so that  the system is highly available, is accessible worldwide and can  be scaled to handle the high traffic. Docker is used to  containerize   application   services   to   ensure   a   similar  environment is used in both development and production  systems.   The   process   of   orchestration   of   containers   is  managed by Kubernetes, which automat es the processes of  deployment,   load   balancing   and   horizontal   scaling   in  accordance with the demand of the user.  Metafiles   such as 3D models and panoramas get stored in a  cloud storage and delivered using content delivery networks  (CDNs). This is achieved by storing assets in edge servers  located in geographically distributed locations so that the  system minimizes latency and   allows a minimum of smooth  streaming   of   immersive   data   to   devices   with   limited  processing capabilities .  V.   R ESULTS AND   D ISCUSSION  The new virtual tourism platform has been tested based on  quantitative   performance   indicators   as   well   as   feedback  generated by the users. The experimental findings revealed  that the adaptive tour flow greatly improved user interaction  as   opposed   to   the   nor mal   non - interactive   virtual   tours.  Customers took about 30 - 50% of the time on exploring  adaptive environments, and the average session length went  up to almost 12 minutes in comparison with 8 minutes before.  There was also an increase in the completion rat es of the tours  by approximately 35 per cent, indicating the increased interest  of   the   user   and   their   engagement.   Performance   In   the  perspective related to system performance, the cloud - based  architecture was able to sustain latency under 100 ms, allowing  scene transitions to occur smoothly and rendering to remain  stable, and Web   XR visualization was able to maintain a  steady frame rate of 50 - 60 fps. The use of user surveys also  supported the use of dynamic navigation and the virtual guide  as it enhanced interactivity and informational value. The  analysis of ablation revealed a sig nificant loss of engagement  in the case of disabling adaptive navigation or guidance  functionality. The system proved to have definite benefits in  the accessibility, responsiveness, and personalized discovery  despite some slight difficulties caused by the   differences in  compatibility with browsers and the use of quality training  information.  VI.   C ONCLUSION  This paper presented a cloud - enabled virtual tourism  platform for personalized experience of travel experiences by  merging   immersive   web   technologies   with   artificial  intelligence   technologies   to   develop   a   virtual   tourism  experience that delivers tailored t raveling experiences directly  through a standard web browser. By leveraging Web   XR and  WebGL for real - time rendering and taking advantage of the  potential   of   the   scalable   cloud   infrastructure   to   deploy  computational processes, the system removes the requirement  for specialist VR equipment whilst still being able to deliver  high - qualit y   visual   immersion.   The   combination   of  behavioural   analytics, hybrid recommendation models, and  adaptive   tour   flow   mechanisms   enables   the   platform   to  dynamically   adapt   navigation   paths   according   to   user  interactions,   enabling   a   more   engaging   and   responsive  exploration   environment.   Experimenting   through  ob servations there was found that users spent more time  interacting with adaptive tours compared to traditional static  virtual   tours   -   indicating   improved   engagement   and  satisfaction. Overall, the proposed framework illustrates that

intelligent, browser - based virtual tourism systems can be  feasible and accessible alternatives to the conventional VR  applications, increasing the outreach of immersive tourism to  a   wider   audience   and   supporting   education,   cultural  preservation, and digita l heritage initiatives .  VII.   FUTURE WORK  Although the proposed system shows the effectiveness of  adaptive   virtual   tourism   over   the   cloud   environment  infrastructure, there are still several opportunities to make  improvements.   Future   research   effort   can   be   put   into  enhancing the visual realism by c ombining high - resolution  photogrammetry technology, dynamic lighting and spatial  audio to build more real - life virtual environment. The adaptive  tour engine can further be made more strong by including  more sophisticated reinforcement learning models that   can  capture long - term user preferences and contextual behavior  patterns across multiple sessions. Additionally, the privacy -  preserving learning techniques like federated learning would  enable personalization while protecting the sensitive user  interaction   data. Expanding the platform to enable real - time  multi - user exploration and cooperative guided tours could  have a significant impact on the social aspect of virtual  tourism.   Integration   with   data   sets   on   live   tourism,  multilingual conversational agents and   accessibility features  for differently - abled users would allow for an even broader  applicability of the system.
